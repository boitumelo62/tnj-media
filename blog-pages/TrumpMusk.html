Media Trump and Musk at odd ends

June 6, 2025 ● Tebuho Zongwana

A public rift between President Donald Trump and tech billionaire Elon Musk intensified on Thursday, as both figures exchanged sharp criticisms over recent policy decisions and their political alliance.

The discord surfaced during a meeting between President Trump and German Chancellor Friedrich Merz at the White House. Trump expressed disappointment in Musk's criticism of the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act," a significant spending package championed by the administration. The bill includes substantial cuts to electric vehicle subsidies, a move that Musk, CEO of Tesla, has vocally opposed.

In response, Musk took to X (formerly Twitter), accusing Trump of "ingratitude" and asserting that the former president would not have won the 2024 election without his support. Musk further alleged that Trump was implicated in the Jeffrey Epstein files, suggesting that the release of these documents had been halted due to Trump's involvement.

The fallout has had immediate financial repercussions. Tesla's stock experienced a significant decline, dropping nearly 15%—its worst day since the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic. Conversely, shares of Trump Media & Technology Group fell 8%, and the $TRUMP cryptocurrency saw a 12% decrease.

Political analysts view this escalation as a pivotal moment in the relationship between the two influential figures. The feud underscores the complexities of their alliance and its potential impact on their respective ventures and political ambitions.

As the situation develops, both Trump and Musk remain central figures in American politics and business, with their public dispute drawing widespread attention.
