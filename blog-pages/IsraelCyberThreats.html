Media Israel Warns Citizens to Disable Home Security Cameras Amid Cyber Threats

June 23, 2025 ● Tebuho Zongwana

The Israeli government has issued a warning to its citizens, urging them to turn off internet-connected home security cameras due to concerns about possible Iranian cyberattacks.

Officials say that these devices, especially smart doorbell cameras and surveillance systems, could be hacked and used to gather intelligence or assist in planning physical attacks. According to Israel's National Cyber Directorate, the primary concern is that foreign actors might exploit camera footage to monitor movements or assess damage following missile strikes.

This alert follows increasing tension between Israel and Iran, including both physical conflict and digital warfare. Authorities fear that Iranian-backed hackers may attempt to use security systems to gain situational awareness during attacks or manipulate footage to spread misinformation.

The Cyber Directorate emphasized that citizens should disconnect these devices from the internet during times of heightened security risks. They also recommended securing any necessary cameras with strong passwords and keeping software updated.

This isn't the first time smart devices have raised security concerns. Past incidents have shown how surveillance technology can be weaponized by adversaries, prompting growing calls worldwide for stricter cybersecurity on consumer tech.
