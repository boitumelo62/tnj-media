Media Salmonella Cases Hit Ten-Year High in England, Officials Warn

England is experiencing its highest number of salmonella infections in over a decade, public health officials have confirmed.

The UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) reported a sharp rise in cases across the country, with over 1,200 confirmed infections so far this year—more than double the average for this time period. The spike is being linked to several ongoing outbreaks, with contaminated food products under investigation.

Dr. Thomas Waite, Deputy Chief Medical Officer, said the trend is “concerning” and urged the public to take basic hygiene precautions, especially during warmer months when foodborne illnesses tend to increase.

“Simple steps like cooking poultry thoroughly, avoiding raw eggs in recipes, and washing hands before and after handling food can make a big difference,” he said.

While most salmonella infections cause stomach cramps, diarrhoea, and fever lasting a few days, they can be more dangerous for young children, older adults, and those with weakened immune systems.

The UKHSA has not yet named all the sources under review but confirmed investigations are ongoing into multiple clusters linked to pre-packaged foods and imported ingredients.

Consumers are advised to check food recalls regularly and report any symptoms of food poisoning to their GP.

This marks the highest salmonella case count in England since 2015. Health officials say a combination of improved reporting and genuine spikes may be driving the increase.

More updates are expected as investigations continue.
