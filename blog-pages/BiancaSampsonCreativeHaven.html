Lifestyle Bianca Sampson Builds a Creative Haven for Young Artists in Johannesburg

June 21, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Breaking into the art world has never been easy, and for many young artists, the road to recognition is filled with obstacles — limited access, financial barriers, and a lack of meaningful connections.

But for 41-year-old Johannesburg businesswoman Bianca Sampson, these challenges are not just distant memories — they're part of her story. Now, she's using her experience to pave the way for others.

“It's tough out there,” Bianca says with a knowing smile. “I feel like the creative industry can be so insular. It's almost like a private club. If you don't know the right people, or you don't have the funds to promote yourself, your work can stay invisible no matter how talented you are.”

Bianca is the founder and driving force behind Neethling & Co, a lifestyle concept store nestled in the heart of Greenside, Johannesburg. But it's more than just a boutique — it's an artistic ecosystem, a place where fashion, visual art, music, and conversation all collide.

And Bianca has made it her mission to ensure that young, often-overlooked creatives get their moment in the spotlight.

Stepping into Neethling & Co is like stepping into Bianca's mind: bright, curious, and bursting with artistic flair. Crisp white walls serve as a gallery for ever-changing works by local painters and sculptors. Mirrors reflect bursts of color, while shelves hold handmade ceramics and statement décor pieces. Mannequins wear bold, original fashion designs, and clothing rails display pieces that seem more at home on a runway than in a store.

But it's the atmosphere that truly sets the space apart. Tables are often occupied by groups deep in discussion — designers brainstorming their next collection, musicians swapping stories over coffee, or artists sketching ideas onto napkins. It's a place where ideas are not only welcomed but expected.

“I wanted to create a space where creativity doesn't just exist on the walls — it lives and breathes in every corner,” Bianca explains.

This Sunday marks an exciting milestone: Bianca will host her first public exhibition at the store, an open-invite showcase that brings together a small but powerful group of emerging artists. From a soulful saxophone player to a boundary-pushing painter, the lineup represents the diversity and raw talent that so often go unnoticed.

“It's not about selling something,” Bianca insists. “It's about giving these artists the chance to be seen, to be heard — to be taken seriously.”

For Bianca, the exhibition is only the beginning. She hopes to make it a regular platform where young creatives can grow, connect, and build the confidence they need to pursue their passion on their own terms.

She's proof that success in the art world doesn't have to come from privilege or connections — it can come from heart, hard work, and a deep commitment to lifting others as you rise.

With Neethling & Co, Bianca Sampson isn't just running a business — she's building a movement. And the art world is better for it.
