Microsoft Slashes 9,000 Jobs in Major Restructure, Xbox Division Hard Hit

Microsoft has announced a sweeping round of layoffs affecting 9,000 employees, roughly 4% of its global workforce. The cuts mark the company's second significant downsizing this year and include major impacts across its gaming division, particularly Xbox Game Studios.

Despite reporting strong user engagement in its gaming sector, Microsoft has opted to streamline its operations, citing a need to reduce management layers and enhance agility. Employees in the Xbox division were among the hardest hit, with an estimated 2,000 roles eliminated. Internal communications indicated that key projects would continue, while affected staff were encouraged to apply for other positions within the company.

Several major game projects were cancelled as part of the restructuring. These include the long-awaited title Everwild from Rare, as well as the reboot of Perfect Dark. The closure of The Initiative, the studio behind Perfect Dark, has also been confirmed. An unannounced MMORPG from ZeniMax Online Studios was also shelved.

The job cuts come amid Microsoft's broader strategic pivot toward artificial intelligence. The company has significantly increased its investments in AI technologies and infrastructure, planning to spend tens of billions of dollars over the next year. Executives have stated that AI now contributes a sizable portion of the company's internal codebase and product development workflows.

This move follows an earlier wave of approximately 6,000 job reductions earlier in 2025. While severance packages and support services have been offered to affected employees, the layoffs reflect growing pressure within the tech industry to cut costs while reorienting around AI and cloud computing.

Microsoft has affirmed that strong-performing teams and projects will remain a priority, even as it undergoes this major organizational shift.
