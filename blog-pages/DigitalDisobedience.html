Media A New Era of Digital Disobedience

June 23, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Turning Ads into Experiences That Stick

Tech News

Samsung to introduce AI technology to their fridges

AI is changing marketing helping teams move faster, target better, and make smarter choices. But when the data is flawed or the goals are wrong, things can go sideways.In early 2025, Paramount dropped a movie trailer with a fully AI-generated voiceover. Instead of praise, it got mocked online for sounding fake.

Around the same time, Activision used AI visuals for Guitar Hero ads. Fans hated them they looked flat and lifeless. Both brands pulled the content fast.

These flops show a key lesson ,if AI content feels robotic, people notice and trust drops. Automation should support creativity, not replace it. Part of the problem is poor goal setting. AI follows instructions exactly. If you aim for clicks but ignore quality or brand impact, you'll get empty results. One retailer focused only on “add-to-cart” rates until abandoned carts spiked.

When they added better KPIs like checkout rates and satisfaction, performance improved. This proves KPIs need to match real business goals and be shared clearly across teams. Goals should evolve too. Early TikTok ads chased views, but short videos didn't build brand recall. When brands shifted focus to “dwell time,” results improved.

Even with good data, AI still needs human help. It can't always catch tone or culture issues. A strong review process testing with real users, logging changes, and slow rollouts can prevent public mistakes like Paramount's.

Cross team collaboration also helps. When marketers and data teams review AI content together, errors are spotted sooner, and quality improves. Benchmarking is another must. Without it, you don't know if AI is improving. Gold standard tests and small sample rollouts help avoid bigger risks. Sharing benchmark results across teams also builds trust and reduces surprises.

In the end, AI doesn't replace marketing basics it needs them. With clear goals, human checks, and strong standards, AI can be a powerful partner not a liability. It's not just about the tech. It's about the choices we make in how we guide it. When done right, AI helps brands grow in ways that feel real and meaningful.
