Business Major Business Shuts Down in South Africa

June 10, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Goodyear has officially announced that it will be shutting down its manufacturing operations in Nelson Mandela Bay, a move that the workers' union NUMSA has described as a serious blow to jobs in the Eastern Cape.

The factory, which has been running since 1947, currently employs around 900 people directly. It also supports many more jobs through services linked to the plant, such as catering, security, transport, and maintenance.

This closure is part of a bigger restructuring effort across Goodyear's Europe, Middle East, and Africa (EMEA) region, as the company adapts to changing market demands and reshapes its business.

Although Goodyear will still have a sales and distribution presence in South Africa and continue operating its Hi-Q retail stores, it will no longer manufacture tyres locally.

Goodyear South Africa is part of the global Goodyear Tyre & Rubber Holdings and plays a role in the company's Emerging Markets group. It has been known for producing a wide range of tyres for everything from cars and SUVs to trucks, off-road vehicles, and even airplanes.

The company's decision kicks off a formal restructuring process, following the rules of South Africa's Labour Relations Act. The Commission for Conciliation, Mediation, and Arbitration (CCMA) will help manage the process. Goodyear has said it is committed to treating affected workers fairly and providing support during this difficult transition.
