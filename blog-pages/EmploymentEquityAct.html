Media Understanding the Employment Equity Amendment Act 2025: Key Changes and Compliance Strategies

June 27, 2025 ● Tebuho Zongwana

South Africa's employment equity framework underwent a significant transformation with the Employment Equity Amendment Act 4 of 2022, which came into force on 1 January 2025. This legislation introduces impactful changes aimed at accelerating workforce transformation and addressing the ongoing underrepresentation of designated groups in key sectors and senior roles.

Key Changes Introduced

The most notable change is the expanded authority granted to the Minister of Employment and Labour, who can now set sector-specific numerical targets for designated groups across various industries and job levels. These targets, released on 15 April 2025 after stakeholder consultations, span 18 economic sectors, outlining demographic benchmarks intended to reflect South Africa's Economically Active Population (EAP) over time.

Designated employers—defined as those with 50 or more employees, regardless of turnover—are now legally required to align their employment equity plans with these targets. This removes the previous turnover threshold and eases reporting burdens on smaller businesses, placing the compliance focus squarely on larger employers.

Failure to meet targets may result in Department of Labour scrutiny. Employers must then provide valid explanations for falling short, reinforcing the state's commitment to measurable progress in equity transformation.

Compliance Certification Requirement

Another major development is the introduction of the Employment Equity Compliance Certificate as a prerequisite for doing business with the State. To qualify, employers must:

  • Demonstrate alignment with sector-specific targets
  • Submit annual Employment Equity reports
  • Have no recent violations related to unfair discrimination or the national minimum wage.

Without this certificate, companies become ineligible for government contracts, posing potential risks to revenue and competitiveness in public procurement.

A Strategic Path Forward

While sectoral targets must be met by 31 August 2030, the Act encourages employers to set measurable annual milestones to ensure gradual, manageable progress. These interim goals help track transformation, facilitate regular adjustments, and foster a culture of continuous improvement.

This incremental approach allows for timely course corrections and avoids the pitfalls of last-minute compliance. Employers are urged to embed employment equity into their long-term strategies, using each year's achievements to build momentum toward the five-year targets.

Importantly, the Act also recognises that full compliance may not always be feasible within a specific timeframe. In such cases, it provides avenues for justification, which will be explored in a follow-up article.

By aligning their internal policies with these national goals, employers can contribute meaningfully to a more inclusive and representative South African workforce.
