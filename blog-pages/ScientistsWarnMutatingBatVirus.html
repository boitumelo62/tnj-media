Media Scientists Warn Mutating Bat Virus Could Lead to Another Pandemic

June 24, 2025 ● Tebuho Zongwana

Researchers are sounding the alarm over a rapidly evolving bat virus that may have the potential to infect humans and cause another global health crisis.

Known as Khosta-2, the virus was first discovered in Russian horseshoe bats and is part of the same viral family as SARS-CoV-2, which caused the COVID-19 pandemic. A recent study found that Khosta-2 can attach to human cells using the ACE2 receptor — the same gateway SARS-CoV-2 uses to enter the body.

Even more concerning is that this virus appears resistant to current COVID-19 vaccines and antibody treatments, meaning that the protections built during the last pandemic may not be effective if Khosta-2 ever spreads to humans.

"This virus isn't currently spreading among people, but it has the genetic makeup and adaptability to make that leap," said researchers involved in the study. They warn that focusing solely on known threats like SARS-CoV-2 could leave us vulnerable to other emerging viruses in wildlife, particularly in bats, which are known reservoirs for many coronaviruses.

The discovery highlights the importance of broad-spectrum vaccines and a global surveillance system for viruses with pandemic potential — especially those circulating in animal populations.
