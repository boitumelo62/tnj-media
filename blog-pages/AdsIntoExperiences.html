Media Turning Ads into Experiences That Stick

Gamification has quietly woven itself into our daily lives, from corporate learning tools to wellness programs like Discovery's Vitality. At its core, the concept is simple: turn routine tasks into engaging games using rewards, points, challenges, or levels. This approach taps into basic human motivation, making tasks feel more enjoyable and driving better participation.

Marketers are increasingly using gamification to cut through the noise in digital advertising. In a space where standard ads often blend into the background, interactive experiences stand out. Done right, gamified ads can boost engagement by nearly 50%, improve brand recall, and build stronger emotional connections with consumers.

Gamified advertising comes in many forms. A simple example is a campaign Puma ran in Malaysia, where users could spin a digital wheel to win a discount. The results were impressive: lead submissions jumped by over 230%, coupon usage surged, and conversion rates saw a noticeable lift. On the more elaborate end, Louis Vuitton created an immersive mystery game on Discord, where users solved daily riddles in a branded digital experience.

This campaign didn't just drive engagement it deepened brand loyalty and showcased the brand's creative identity in the digital world. Between these two extremes, gamified ads can take on a wide range of formats. From swipeable product reveals to quizzes, virtual rooms, and interactive videos, the options are limited only by creative vision and budget. A rising trend is banners that mimic a Google Shopping feed but with a twist like clickable furniture in a virtual room that leads to more product information or purchase pages. Despite the potential, many brands still rely on basic banner ads.

The main reason is cost. Gamified ads can be expensive to produce and serve, especially when they include complex graphics, animations, and interactive elements. Rich media ads can cost up to ten times more to run than a standard banner on platforms like Google Campaign Manager 360. Marketers have to weigh whether the improved engagement is worth the higher investment.

There's also the challenge of capturing user attention. The internet is saturated with content, and people won't engage with just any game. It has to be relevant, exciting, and worth their time. A tired spin-the-wheel mechanic or a generic match-three game won't impress users who are used to high-quality gaming apps and social media content. The campaign needs a strong creative idea, real value for the user, and a meaningful brand connection to succeed. Fortunately, things are changing. Costs are starting to come down as AI tools and platforms like Google Studio make it easier and faster to create rich media ads. As production becomes more accessible, more brands will have the opportunity to explore gamified formats.

But even with these advances, gamified ads won't replace traditional banners entirely. Each format plays a role. A balanced strategy that uses interactive ads for high-impact engagement and standard banners for broad, cost-effective reach can deliver the best results. Gamification is a powerful tool when used thoughtfully not just as a gimmick, but as a way to truly connect with audiences and stand out in a crowded digital world.
