Innovation Government Confirms Major Investment in UK Hydrogen Infrastructure and Clean Energy Jobs

June 13, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

The UK government has officially confirmed a major investment in hydrogen infrastructure, marking a significant step forward in the country’s transition to cleaner energy. As part of the latest Spending Review, new funding has been allocated to develop the UK’s first regional hydrogen transport and storage network. This will, for the first time, link hydrogen producers directly with major users such as power stations and heavy industry.

This landmark move is expected to create thousands of skilled jobs across key industrial regions like Merseyside, Teesside, and the Humber, as well as throughout the wider supply chain. It’s not just about energy it’s about rebuilding local economies, securing long-term jobs, and reducing dependence on volatile international fossil fuel markets.

The announcement comes alongside other clean energy job initiatives confirmed in the Spending Review. These include:

10,000 jobs at Sizewell C, a new nuclear plant in Suffolk. A new fusion reactor to be built in Nottinghamshire, creating even more employment opportunities.

Up to 3,000 jobs through the Small Modular Reactor (SMR) programme. Funding for the Acorn project in Scotland and the Viking project in the Humber, further supporting the UK’s low-carbon future.

These initiatives will support industries that rely heavily on hydrogen infrastructure, such as the iron, steel, glass, chemical, and ceramics sectors. This is part of a broader government strategy to future-proof UK manufacturing and energy.

“We are investing over half a billion pounds in our industrial heartlands to deliver jobs and energy security for Britain. By building hydrogen networks, we are securing homegrown energy that will power British industry for generations to come.” Hydrogen is expected to play a key role in the UK’s future energy system. It has the potential to reduce emissions in industries like oil refining and heavy transport, and it can also serve as a long term energy storage solution helping the grid meet demand when renewable sources like wind or solar are less active.

The funding will also support the ongoing Hydrogen Allocation Rounds (HAR), which are driving low carbon hydrogen production across the country. The first round alone awarded over £2 billion to 11 promising projects.

Industry Leaders Back the Plans Experts in the hydrogen sector have welcomed the announcement. Brett Ryan, Head of Policy and Analysis at Hydrogen UK, said:

“Hydrogen networks are essential for a secure and resilient hydrogen sector. Ensuring sufficient energy storage capacity will be critical to energy security and affordability during the energy transition.”

Dr. Emma Guthrie, CEO of the Hydrogen Energy Association, added:

“By investing in transport and storage infrastructure, the government is rightly joining the dots connecting already supported hydrogen production with the industries that need it most.” Private Sector Already On Board Hydrogen infrastructure has already attracted over £400 million in private investment, with key developments in places like Milford Haven in Wales and High Marnham in Nottinghamshire.

Government support is expected to accelerate this momentum creating jobs for apprentices, graduates, engineers, welders, construction workers, pipefitters, and operations specialists across the UK.
