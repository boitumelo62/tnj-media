Since October 2024, there has been much buzz around a hinted 360-degree camera from DJI have been swirling since October. However, there have been a few fresh leaks that could grant ‘intel’ about the much-anticipated DJI Osmo 360 camera – as well as hinting at the specifications it will bring with it.

DJI, short for Dajiang Innovation, is a Chinese company specializing in drones, gimbals, and related equipment. They are known for their innovative and high-quality consumer and professional-grade products, dominating the drone market.

For those new to the tech space, a 360-degree camera also called an omnidirectional camera, captures a 360-degree view of its surroundings. In contrast to traditional cameras that capture a limited field of view, 360 cameras use multiple lenses, typically fisheye lenses, to record an entire sphere of imagery. This allows for immersive, spherical images or videos that viewers can explore from any angle. This camera has also led to the boom of the Virtual Reality (VR) world.

Popular tech geek and tipster @GAtamer (via Notebookcheck) posted pictures of the DJI Osmo 360, displaying the compact camera, the two lenses on the front and back of the device, the small integrated touchscreen, and what looks like an accessory mount.

According to the same source, the specs of the DJI Osmo 360 are going to be "almost the same as the X5", referring of course to the Insta360 X5 that launched in April – another 360-degree camera that the DJI Osmo 360 will be challenging head on.

@GAtamer’s post was a follow-up to another image leaked by @Quadro_News, which seems to show the DJI Osmo 360 in some form of packaging. Again, we can see one of the camera lenses and the shape of the upcoming gadget.

For now, all we can glean from these latest DJI Osmo 360 leaks, and we do not get any information here about a launch date or potential pricing. It seems likely that the camera will be appearing sooner rather than later. However, it is rumoured that the DJI Osmo 360 would be launching in July this, news that will be highly welcomed by the entire tech community.
