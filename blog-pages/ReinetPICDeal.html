Reinet Moves Closer to Major PIC Deal

Reinet Investments, led by Johann Rupert as executive chairman, has spoken out about the potential sale of its largest asset, the UK-based Pension Insurance Corporation Group (PIC). PIC is Reinet's most valuable holding, making up over half of the company's total assets.

Reinet indirectly owns a 49.5% stake in PIC, which specializes in managing pension funds through secure and socially responsible investments. As of the end of 2024, PIC oversaw £50.9 billion (around R1.2 trillion) in assets and managed pensions for nearly 400,000 people.

After recent media reports raised questions about a possible sale, Reinet confirmed that talks are well advanced.

Athora Holdings, a company that provides retirement and insurance services, is negotiating to potentially buy 100% of PIC. The proposed deal values PIC at £5.7 billion (R135 billion), which is higher than previous estimates of up to £5 billion (R120 billion). However, Reinet made it clear that nothing is final yet. There's no guarantee that the deal will go through, and the company promised to update shareholders if and when anything becomes official.

This potential sale would be Reinet's second major move in recent months. At the end of 2024 and into early 2025, the company sold its stake in British American Tobacco (BAT), a decision that significantly boosted its cash reserves. Reinet now holds €1.8 billion (R37 billion) in cash and liquid assets — a big jump from just 5.7% of its portfolio a year ago to 26.3% now.

Johann Rupert highlighted in the company's latest financial results that this strong cash position puts Reinet in a solid place to fund current and future investments. It also strengthens the broader Rupert business empire, which includes major holdings in Remgro and luxury group Richemont.
