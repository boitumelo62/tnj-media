China Achieves Milestone in Hypersonic Flight With “Feitian-2” Test

China’s Northwestern Polytechnical University has successfully tested a new hypersonic aircraft, known as Feitian-2, reportedly reaching speeds of around Mach 12, approximately 14,800 km/h.

The test marks a significant leap in hypersonic research, surpassing the Mach 5 threshold that defines hypersonic speed. According to Chinese media sources, the aircraft uses a hybrid propulsion system, transitioning from a rocket booster to a ramjet engine mid-flight. This seamless propulsion shift represents a major engineering feat, especially given the intense heat and pressure such vehicles endure at extreme speeds.

Feitian-2 is said to operate autonomously, piloting itself through different phases of the flight without external control. While technical specifics such as: its size, range, or intended use—remain classified, the successful transition and sustained flight at hypersonic speeds could have both military and civilian implications.

The announcement arrives as concerns grow globally over the strategic use of hypersonic weapons. These systems are difficult to detect and intercept due to their high speed, maneuverability, and low flight altitude, posing new challenges for existing missile defense technologies.

China’s successful test follows recent U.S. efforts to limit China’s access to advanced aerospace components through tightened export controls. Despite these restrictions, the demonstration of Feitian-2 shows China is advancing its hypersonic capabilities and pursuing greater technological independence in the aerospace sector.
