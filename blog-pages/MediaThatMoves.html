Media Media That Moves Markets

June 30, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Why Media and Creative Must Work Together Again

An outsourced digital media agency could be the missing piece for many South African brands big or small trying to get ahead in an increasingly competitive online landscape. South Africa's digital advertising spend is expected to hit over $204 million in 2025, and with more than 50 million internet users and mobile penetration nearing 84%, the opportunity for businesses to grow through online platforms is massive.

But the potential for growth doesn't mean results are guaranteed. As Cheryl Ingram, CEO of The Digital Media Collective (TDMC), and her team stress, if brands don't prioritise a strong, strategic paid media presence, they risk falling behind. Caleb Shepard, TDMC's Media Director, puts it bluntly: with Google dominating 99% of the paid search market in South Africa, this isn't an area where businesses can afford to take shortcuts or experiment blindly. The numbers back this up. South Africans now spend an average of 9.5 hours online every day, the highest globally according to the WeAreSocial 2025 Global Report. That's a huge opportunity especially for e-commerce but only if businesses can meet consumers with relevant, timely, and strategically placed content.

Despite this, many companies still believe they'll save money by keeping digital media planning and buying in-house. But that assumption often hides the real costs. Shepard points out that these businesses usually see poorer performance and end up losing far more in missed opportunities than they ever save in salaries. One big issue is what Ingram calls the “unicorn syndrome” trying to find one person who can do it all: strategy, content, paid media, emails, analytics, leadership reporting, and more. It's a common mistake, and it nearly always leads to underperformance or burnout. The digital world is simply too complex now for one generalist to cover every base well. It's not just unrealistic it's often a recipe for poor results.

Agencies like TDMC offer something in-house teams rarely can: access to a full team of experts, each specialising in different areas of digital media. Shepard calls this “paying for the full brain at a fraction of the cost.” TDMC's credentials back that up they're Shopify Select Partners, a Google Premier Partner for three years running, and recognised by Meta, TikTok and Klaviyo too. This range of partnerships means they have direct access to support, training, and inside knowledge that gives them and their clients a real edge.While hiring an agency may seem more expensive on the surface, when you look at the depth of services, tools, and knowledge you gain access to, the value is far greater than trying to build or manage an internal team with similar capabilities. Paid media alone is so layered and technical that a single person simply can't keep up with it all.

On TDMC's teams, you'll find some of the country's most experienced specialists across platforms. They also benefit from being in constant communication with partners like Google and Meta, which means they're often first to know when algorithm changes or new features roll out and they can pivot quickly, often before those changes impact results.

Another major advantage is the exposure agencies have across different industries and client types. This daily immersion in a broad mix of accounts helps agency teams pick up on trends, troubleshoot faster, and develop creative strategies that someone working in house on a single brand might never encounter.

In contrast, when businesses go in house, they usually limit themselves to just one or two digital hires. Those individuals may be skilled, but they don't benefit from the peer learning, collaboration, or constant upskilling that happens inside an agency environment. And unless hiring managers themselves have deep digital expertise, there's a high risk of bringing in underqualified candidates who can't keep pace with how fast the landscape shifts.

Beyond skills and experience, there are hidden risks when companies try to handle digital media in-house. HR challenges, extra overheads, and long-term liabilities all fall on the business. Shepard highlights how many executives still misunderstand digital marketing seeing it as a quick fix or a secondary priority rather than the critical growth driver it actually is.

Agencies help shoulder the risk. Their relationships with platforms and their ability to quickly shift strategies without the roadblocks of internal bureaucracy or narrow bandwidth make them nimble and responsive something increasingly necessary in today's fast moving environment.

At TDMC, agility is a core part of how they work. With more than 120 clients across industries, their team is constantly testing, refining, and learning.

That “always on” approach gives clients the peace of mind that their strategy is being monitored and adjusted in real time, with insight driven decisions guiding every move. Ingram sums it up well, once businesses see the real value an agency brings, the idea that it costs more than hiring a person or two internally quickly fades. The results speak for themselves.
