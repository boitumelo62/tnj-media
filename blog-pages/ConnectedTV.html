Connected TV Blends TV's Impact with Digital Precision

Connected TV (CTV) isn't something new; it's just the next step in how we watch TV. Instead of traditional broadcast or satellite, CTV delivers TV shows and movies through the internet on smart devices. For advertisers, this means bigger budgets, better targeting, and viewers who are more engaged.

People watch CTV differently from regular TV. With old TV, channels just play shows automatically. With CTV, viewers choose what, when, and where to watch from a menu of apps. This makes watching more intentional and focused, which is great for advertisers because viewers pay more attention.

Most CTV content is long-form and viewed on big screens, creating a premium setting for ads that really connect with audiences. While traditional TV had channel surfing, today's viewers switch between apps like Netflix, YouTube, and Showmax. This app-switching means more choice but also more fragmentation, so getting noticed early—like on the smart TV home screen—is key.

CTV blends the best of TV and digital: it has TV's impact with digital's precision. Advertisers can target audiences specifically, track results, and tweak campaigns quickly, all within a familiar TV environment. In places like South Africa, ad-supported streaming is growing, making CTV even more important. Even though how people watch TV has changed, media planning hasn't caught up yet.

CTV is often squeezed into digital budgets or ignored, missing a big opportunity. Today's viewers move seamlessly between linear, pay, and connected TV, so plans should reflect that. CTV isn't just another channel. It's TV evolved—measurable, precise, and ready for serious investment.
