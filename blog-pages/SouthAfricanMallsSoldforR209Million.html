June 6, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Collins Property Group has sold three of its South African shopping centres for a total of R209 million, as part of a plan to reinvest in the Netherlands.

The malls sold are the Nquthu Shopping Centre, Ezulwini Royal Shopping Centre (both in Ulundi), and the Eyethu Junction Shopping Centre. Two were sold by Collins' subsidiary Imbali Props 21 for R106 million, and the third by Colkru Investments, a 90%-owned subsidiary, for R103 million all purchased by Fairvest.

The malls generated nearly R20 million in combined net income over the past year. Collins, a JSE-listed Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT), said the sale aligns with its strategy to recycle capital and grow its European footprint.

Founded in 1904, Collins Property Group owns more than 120 properties across Southern Africa and Europe. The company emphasized that the sale prices reflect fair market value and do not require shareholder approval.
