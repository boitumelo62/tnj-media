Business FIA Green Lights Kyalami Upgrades in Bold Move Toward Formula 1 Return

June 19, 2025 ● Tebuho Zongwana

South Africa's historic Kyalami Grand Prix Circuit has taken a monumental step toward re claiming its place on the global motorsport stage. The FIA has officially approved upgrade plans that would elevate the circuit from its current Grade 2 status to all important Grade 1—making it eligible to host Formula 1 races once again.

Only Grade 1 circuits meet the safety, infrastructure, and facility standards mandated by Formula 1. With the FIA's sign off on final design proposals, Kyalami now has a three year window to complete enhancements such as advanced run off zones, high grade barriers, reinforced debris fencing, re engineered kerbs, and upgraded drainage systems.

These upgrades are described by Apex Circuit Design's founder Clive Bowen—who also designed Miami International Autodrome—as a “light touch” approach. There will be no changes to the existing circuit layout, preserving Kyalami's legendary character and flow.

Estimated to cost around $10 million (roughly R180-200 million), the project reflects a strategic, phased execution plan aimed at minimizing disruption to ongoing events held at the venue. Circuit owner Toby Venter, who purchased Kyalami in 2014, described the approval as “a defining moment for South African motorsport” and marks “a bold new chapter” toward bringing back the roar of F1 engines to African soil.

If successfully upgraded and selected by Formula 1 management, Kyalami would become the only Grade 1 circuit on the entire African continent. It's part of South Africa's bid, with competing venues emerging (e.g., Cape Town Street circuit), to host a return F1 Grand Prix in the post 2026 era.

The African continent hasn't seen a Formula 1 race since 1993, when the final South African GP was held at Kyalami. Recent efforts by figures like Lewis Hamilton and discussions with African nations such as Rwanda and Morocco highlight the growing momentum to re introduce F1 to this passionate region.

Timeline & Next Moves

  • 2014: Toby Venter acquires Kyalami, begins push for revival
  • 2016: Venue upgraded to Grade 2
  • 2025: FIA approves Grade 1 design; three year timeline begins
  • 2026-2028: Potential completion window; pending F1 calendar inclusion

Final construction will be staged to avoid conflicts with current events, with some works contingent upon South Africa securing an F1 slot and Kyalami being chosen as the hosting venue
