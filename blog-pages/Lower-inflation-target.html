Finance Lower inflation target offers relief to indebted consumers

June 2, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Reserve Bank Governor Lesetja Kganyago says setting a lower inflation target could help consumers who are struggling with debt by reducing the amount they pay on their loans. It could also boost the economy and create more jobs.

According to Kganyago, lower inflation would lead to interest rates that are not only lower but also more stable over time. This would make it cheaper to borrow money for things like home loans, car finance, or small business investments.

Just yesterday, the Reserve Bank's Monetary Policy Committee cut the interest rate by 25 basis points, bringing the repo rate down to 7.25%. As a result, the prime lending rate which is the rate commercial banks use when lending to their best customers has dropped to 10.75%

Kganyago stressed that the plan to shift to a lower inflation target is still in progress. Once the Bank settles on what that target should be, it will recommend it to the Finance Minister for approval.

“What the numbers show,” Kganyago explained, “is that with lower inflation and interest rates, the economy would grow more. And when the economy grows, that means more opportunities for jobs. So it's not just good for borrowers it's good for everyone.”
