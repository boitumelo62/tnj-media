Lifestyle Social media is making eating disorders worse, experts warn.

June 4, 2025

Apps like TikTok and Instagram flood young users with unrealistic beauty standards, promoting extreme thinness and dangerous dieting advice. Experts say this fuels eating disorders such as anorexia, bulimia, and binge eating especially in young women and girls, though more men are affected now too.

Globally, the number of people with eating disorders more than doubled between 2000 and 2018, a period that aligns with the rise of social media.

French nutritionist Carole Copti says treating an eating disorder now always involves looking at social media use. “It's a trigger, an accelerator, and a barrier to recovery,” she said.

Though eating disorders have many causes psychological, genetic, and social media often pushes vulnerable people over the edge. Trends like #skinnytok on TikTok promote extreme diets and guilt around eating, worsening the issue.

Eating disorders can cause serious health problems, including heart damage, infertility, and even death. Anorexia has the highest death rate of any psychiatric illness.

Social media can trap people in a cycle, Copti explained: “Low self-esteem leads them to share images of their thinness, which gets them likes and attention reinforcing the illness instead of helping them heal.”
