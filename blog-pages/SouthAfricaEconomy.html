Finance Economists Predict Flat Growth for South Africa's Economy in Early 2025

June 4, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Standard Bank economist Dr Elna Moolman says she doesn't expect South Africa's economy to have grown in the first quarter of 2025. This follows a modest 0.6% growth in the final quarter of last year, which helped the country narrowly avoid a recession.

Statistics South Africa is expected to release the official figures for the first quarter later today. Recent numbers from key sectors including mining, manufacturing, and electricity have pointed to a struggling economy. Agriculture, however, is expected to be one of the few bright spots.

Moolman says the weak performance is largely due to uncertainty during the first few months of the year.

“There were two main sources of uncertainty,” she explains. “Firstly, ongoing concerns about U.S. trade policy and potential tariff changes, which could have serious global consequences. And secondly, domestic uncertainty around South Africa's budget earlier this year, especially in February and March, which at one point threatened the stability of the Government of National Unity.”
