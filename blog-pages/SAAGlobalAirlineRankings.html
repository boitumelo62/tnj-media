Business South African Airways Makes Progress in Global Airline Rankings

June 19, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

South African Airways (SAA) has once again secured a spot among the top 100 airlines in the world, moving up to 67th place in the 2025 Skytrax World Airline Awards.

That's a slight but steady improvement from 69th place in 2024 and a positive sign of the airline's continued recovery.

These rankings are based on the world's largest annual airline passenger survey, which isn't limited to certain airlines. Anyone can vote, and it reflects real customer choices from all over the globe. This year's results included over 325 airlines, with the top 100 published.

SAA's steady rise in the rankings comes as the airline works its way through a long-term turnaround plan. After years of financial trouble and bailouts, made worse by the pandemic in 2020, the airline has finally begun to turn a corner. In fact, SAA posted a net profit of R252 million for the 2022/23 financial year the first profit since 2012.

As part of its recovery, the airline is focusing on consolidating its operations, opening new routes, and improving both service and financial stability.Meanwhile, other South African carriers also made a mark in the low-cost category. FlySafair was named the best low-cost airline in Africa, beating out last year's winner, LIFT. Fastjet also made it into the top three in this category.

FlySafair's Chief Marketing Officer, Kirby Gordon, said the recognition is a testament to the team's hard work. “We've spent over a decade committed to providing South Africans with affordable, reliable travel and it's great to see that effort recognised globally.”

In other categories, Airlink claimed the title of the cleanest airline in Africa this year, overtaking SAA. Airlink also ranked as the best regional airline on the continent. However, when it comes to staff service, South African airlines still trail behind some of their African peers. SAA ranked 4th in Africa for airline staff, followed by Airlink (7th), LIFT (8th), and FlySafair (10th). The top spots went to EgyptAir, RwandAir, and Ethiopian Airlines

These awards highlight the growing competitiveness of African airlines and show that South Africa's carriers are continuing to make progress with some already earning recognition on the global stage
