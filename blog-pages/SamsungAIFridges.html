Samsung has announced a new software update to introduce new features to its Bespoke AI series refrigerators. This update will bring Bixby improvements, allowing it to offer a customized user experience based on voice recognition. It will also bring Samsung TV Plus to Bespoke AI refrigerators with AI Home screens in more countries.

Today, Samsung announced that it will soon release a new software update to its Bespoke AI refrigerators with Family Hub screens and AI Home screens. The update, which comes to Bespoke AI refrigerators with Family Hub screens in the first quarter of this year, introduces Bixby's Voice ID feature that lets refrigerators identify a person via their voice and offer them a customized experience. Bixby is a virtual assistant developed by Samsung. It's designed to simplify interactions with your Samsung device, allowing you to control various functions, from opening apps to setting alarms, using voice commands or a dedicated Bixby button. Bixby learns and adapts to your preferences, offering a personalized experience as you use it more.

Bixby uses voice recognition to detect which family member is currently using the refrigerator, and then automatically switches to the associated user account. For this feature to work, the user's voice needs to be registered with their Samsung Account on the refrigerator or on their own Galaxy smartphone or tablet.

Refrigerators with AI Home screens (7-inch or 9-inch) are getting access to Samsung TV Plus in more countries. Currently, the feature is available only in South Korea and the USA. In the second quarter of this year, the live TV and on-demand video streaming service will be made available on Samsung Bespoke AI refrigerators with AI Home screens in Australia, Brazil, Canada, India, and Mexico.
