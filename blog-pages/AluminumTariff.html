Finance US Steel, Aluminium Tariff Hikes to Take Effect Wednesday

June 4, 2025

The United States is set to double tariffs on imported steel and aluminum starting Wednesday, according to an order released by the White House and signed by former President Donald Trump. This move will raise the existing tariffs on these metals from 25% to 50%.

While the higher rates will apply broadly, imports from the United Kingdom will still be subject to the original 25% tariff for now. Both countries are continuing negotiations around duties and quotas based on a previous trade agreement.

According to the official order, the goal of the tariff hike is to better protect U.S. steel and aluminum industries from foreign competition particularly from countries that flood the American market with cheap, excess supply. The administration argues that such practices undermine the competitiveness of U.S. manufacturers and could pose national security risks.

“Increasing the tariffs will strengthen these industries and help eliminate the threat caused by imports of steel and aluminum products,” the order stated.

Trump had previewed the decision during a speech at a U.S. Steel plant in Pennsylvania last week, telling workers, “Nobody is going to be able to steal your industry. At 25 percent, they can sort of get over that fence. At 50 percent, they can no longer get over the fence.”

However, the move has sparked concern among key U.S. trading partners. Over the weekend, the European Union warned that it may retaliate, criticizing the sudden tariff hike as a step that “undermines ongoing efforts to reach a negotiated solution” between the EU and the United States.
