Tech News SpaceX's Final Dragon Capsule 'Grace' Launches with Axiom's Ax-4 Crew

June 25, 2025 ● Tebuho Zongwana

SpaceX's last planned Dragon spacecraft, aptly named Grace, launched into orbit Wednesday, marking the beginning of Axiom Space's Ax-4 mission to the International Space Station.

Lifting off from NASA's Kennedy Space Center, Grace carried a four-person international crew led by veteran astronaut Peggy Whitson. Upon reaching orbit, Whitson, who holds the U.S. record for most time in space, unveiled the capsule's name.

“Grace is more than a name,” Whitson said. “It reflects the elegance with which we move through space... the harmony of science and spirit.”

Whitson is joined by pilot Shubhanshu "Shuks" Shukla (India), and mission specialists Sławosz Uznański-Wiśniewski (Poland) and Tibor Kapu (Hungary)—each representing only the second astronaut from their respective nations to fly in over four decades.

The naming of Grace follows a tradition where first-time crews on new Dragon capsules select a name. Originally slated for NASA's Crew-10, the spacecraft (serial number 213) was reassigned to Ax-4 after a scheduling shift. Crew-10 commander Anne McClain hinted they had their own name picked but passed lobbying efforts on to the next crew.

The name may be inspired by the crew's zero-gravity indicator—a plush swan named Joy—symbolizing the transition “from Joy to Grace,” said Axiom engineer Paul Newton.

Grace now joins the ranks of previous Crew Dragon capsules: Endeavour, Resilience, Endurance, and Freedom—each named to reflect human values and milestones in space exploration.

Ax-4's mission continues the collaboration between commercial partners and NASA in expanding human access to space.
