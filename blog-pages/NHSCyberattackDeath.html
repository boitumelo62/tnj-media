Media Cyberattack Linked to Patient Death in UK as NHS Services Disrupted

June 26, 2025 ● Tebuho Zongwana

A ransomware attack on the UK's National Health Service (NHS) in June 2024 has now been linked to the death of a patient, according to an official review conducted by King's College Hospital NHS Foundation Trust.

The cyberattack, carried out by the Russian-linked group Qilin, targeted Synnovis, a pathology services provider. It disrupted blood testing services across London hospitals and GP offices, forcing the cancellation of over 10,000 appointments and more than 1,000 operations. One direct consequence, investigators say, was a dangerous delay in returning critical blood test results for a patient who later died.

A spokesperson for the trust confirmed that “a number of contributing factors” led to the fatal outcome, including “a long wait for a blood test result due to the cyberattack impacting pathology services at the time.” The conclusion follows mounting concern over the real-world dangers of cyberattacks on public healthcare systems.

Beyond delays in care, the attackers also stole and published nearly 400GB of sensitive patient data, including NHS numbers, names, and medical records. The breach caused a severe shortage of O type blood and left parts of London's healthcare system struggling to operate.

In comments to the BBC, the Qilin group offered a vague apology but denied responsibility, claiming the attack was a form of political retaliation related to an unspecified war involving the UK.

Experts are now calling for deeper scrutiny into the NHS's cybersecurity. Dr. Saif Abed, a former NHS doctor and cybersecurity expert, told the Financial Times that this case may only be “the tip of the iceberg,” and warned that other patient deaths may have gone unreported due to the absence of proper investigations. He urged immediate action in the form of an independent inquiry.

The incident follows a similar case in Germany in 2022, when a cyberattack forced a hospital transfer that delayed treatment and led to the death of a critically ill patient.

As cyber threats grow more sophisticated, healthcare systems around the world are under increasing pressure to strengthen digital defenses and prepare for potentially deadly consequences.
