Seven Steps to Strengthen Your Online Account Security

As cybercrime continues to rise, experts are encouraging individuals to take essential steps to protect their online identities, particularly through social media and email platforms.

One of the first recommendations is to review and minimise your digital footprint. Deleting unused accounts and outdated posts can reduce the risk of exploitation, as older or forgotten profiles may serve as easy targets for hackers.

Using strong, unique passwords for every account is also critical. Cybersecurity specialists advise the use of long and complex phrases or randomly generated strings, ideally stored within a trusted password manager to prevent reuse across platforms.

Two-factor authentication is widely regarded as a vital security measure. By requiring an additional code through an app or device, it adds a second layer of defence, even if a password is compromised.

Adjusting privacy settings on social media accounts can also enhance protection. Limiting who can view personal content, tag you in posts, or access your location helps to safeguard private information from malicious use.

Users are urged to remain cautious of phishing attempts. Suspicious emails or unsolicited messages containing links should be treated with skepticism. It is important to verify the identity of the sender before engaging.

Keeping all software and applications up to date ensures the latest security patches are in place. This helps prevent vulnerabilities from being exploited.

Finally, it is recommended to avoid accessing sensitive accounts over public Wi-Fi. Using a virtual private network and antivirus software can provide additional layers of security.

Online safety requires consistent and informed effort. Each small action contributes to a more secure digital presence.
