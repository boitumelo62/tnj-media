Finance South Africa's Carbon Tax Insufficient to Cut Emissions

June 5, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

The OECD says South Africa's carbon tax is too low to make a real impact on reducing emissions. At the same time, it recognizes that the government struggles to get public and political support to raise taxes further.

In its latest report, the OECD explains that carbon pricing like a carbon tax is one of the most effective and affordable ways to cut emissions. It encourages companies to go green and helps raise money for climate and social projects.

Right now, South Africa's carbon tax is lower than the international average, especially in sectors like electricity and industry. However, higher fuel taxes mean that prices in transport, agriculture, and buildings are above average.

Even though some firms do pay a carbon tax, the actual amount is low, and many emissions aren't taxed at all. This makes it hard for the country to reach its 2030 climate targets.

The government plans to raise the carbon tax each year until 2030 and then quadruple it by 2050. But the OECD warns that the current rates are still too low and will need to rise much more unless other strong climate actions are taken.

The OECD understands that gradual increases may be necessary for now but urges South Africa to stick to its long-term climate plans. A clear and steady policy, it says, will give businesses the confidence to invest in cleaner technologies.
