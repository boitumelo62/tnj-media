Business Takealot Group Posts Another Loss but Aims for Profitability Soon

June 23, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Despite recording another financial loss, the Takealot Group is confident that it will turn a profit in the current financial year. The group's parent company, Naspers, reported that Takealot experienced strong growth in the second half of the 2025 financial year, largely thanks to improved customer services and its growing TakealotMore subscription programme.

According to Naspers' full-year results, Takealot's revenue rose by 20% in US dollar terms, reaching $823 million (about R14 billion). Gross Merchandise Value (GMV) also grew by 13% year-on-year, showing a rebound after a slow start. The group's gross profit margin improved by 1%, supported by high-performing segments like Mr D's grocery delivery service.

However, this revenue growth didn't translate into profits. Takealot posted an adjusted loss before interest and tax (aEBIT) of $12 million (around R217 million). This was mainly due to increased spending on marketing and infrastructure, as the group gears up to face competition from new global players like Amazon, which launched in South Africa last year.

While still in the red, Takealot has narrowed its loss from $14 million the previous year. Naspers remains optimistic, saying the group is still on track to become profitable in the 2026 financial year. Part of its strategy has included selling off Superbalist.com and acquiring M24 Logistics to strengthen delivery capabilities.

Customer loyalty continues to rise, with the TakealotMore programme encouraging more frequent purchases and a growing number of orders. Takealot.com itself saw a 19% revenue increase to $706 million (R12.7 billion), while Mr D's revenue rose by 11% to $117 million (R2.1 billion). Mr D also saw a huge 81% surge in grocery GMV and turned a $4 million profit.

The group has also been investing in greener logistics, expanding its fleet of electric delivery trucks, which is now one of the largest in South Africa. While Takealot is one of the most familiar Naspers brands in South Africa, the broader Naspers group is far more extensive, with most of its value tied to its international holdings most notably, its stake in Chinese tech giant Tencent. Naspers and its Dutch-listed subsidiary Prosus have a combined market value of about R3 trillion.

In the year ending March 2025, Prosus' e-commerce operations delivered a strong performance, with adjusted earnings before interest and tax of $443 million (R8 billion). The group's overall aEBIT jumped to $130 million (R2.3 billion), and core headline earnings climbed 46% to $3.1 billion (R56 billion).

Looking ahead, Naspers CEO Fabricio Bloisi said the company is rapidly evolving into a global tech driven lifestyle e-commerce leader.

Recent acquisitions, including travel platform Despegar and an ongoing deal with Just Eat Takeaway.com, aim to further expand its international footprint and innovation capacity. As a sign of confidence, the Prosus board has recommended doubling its dividend from 10 euro cents to 20 euro cents per share.
