Business Adapt or Die - Why the SABC is collapsing.

What Happens When Millions of South Africans Lose Access to Free-to-Air TV?

The state of the South African Broadcasting Corporation (SABC) has been central topic of discussion for many years now. The company responsible for bringing forth some of South Africa's most nostalgic and groundbreaking shows, has found itself in deep trouble, facing a major collapse. Here we discuss the numerous factors that could very well be the cause of the dismal situation in which it has found itself in.

Collapse of TV Licence Revenue

The rise of streaming services such Amazon Prime and Showmax along with the rise of social media (YouTube in particular), has led to a decline in the compliance rate with regards to TV License payments. This decline has dropped to as low as 14% by 2024. Simply put: South Africans simply do not pay their TV licences, and this is due to a flawed and outdated system that has led to a sharp drop in one of the SABC's key income sources.

Negative effects of Load Shedding

Regular power outages (load shedding) disrupt television and radio broadcasting, which inevitably has led to audiences shrinking during outages, and severely reducing advertising reach and revenue. It has also led to an increase in operational costs such installing backup power, along with the fuel costs to operate such machinery which causes interrupted workflows and productivity.

Over-reliance on Advertising

As it stands, 80% of SABC's revenue comes from advertising, and this model is one that is not sustainable. We all tend to dislike adverts, and cand often find them annoying is they are over played, and that is what the SABC does. During economic downturns or shifts in viewing habits (like people switching to streaming), ad revenue suffers. The limited number of high-performing shows also restricts ad potential.

Low Investment in Content

Due to financial strain, over 80% of its long-term capital plan has been suspended. This includes slashing budgets for added content, which makes it harder to attract and retain audiences.

In conclusion, like the concept of evolution as conceptualized by Charles Darwin himself, it is the survival of the fittest. It is important for companies to keep track of the dynamic media and tech world, to ensure sustainability and longevity.
