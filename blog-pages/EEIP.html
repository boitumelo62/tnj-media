Solly Malatsi, South Africa's Minister of Communications and Digital Technologies since July 2024, has introduced several policy initiatives aimed at modernizing the country's digital landscape and enhancing economic empowerment. In line with the Government of National Unity's mission to maximize prospects of attracting investments through regulatory reforms, Minister Malatsi, published a policy direction on the role of equity equivalent investment programmes (EEIPs) in the ICT sector as a mechanism to accelerate broadband access. Broadband Connectivity as an Empowerment Tool

Malatsi has emphasized broadband connectivity as a central element of economic empowerment. He plans to issue a policy direction to the Independent Communications Authority of South Africa (ICASA) to recognize equity equivalent programs as an alternative to the 30% black ownership requirement for ICT companies. This move aims to attract foreign investment and facilitate the entry of global players like Starlink into the South African market. Additionally, he advocates for lowering regulatory barriers and reducing the cost of smart devices to expand internet access, particularly in underserved and rural areas.

Privatization of the South African Post Office (SAPO)

Facing financial challenges and potential liquidation, SAPO is under review for possible privatization. Malatsi has sought the support of the National Treasury to form a task team that will explore private financial and operational partnerships. The goal is to modernize SAPO's operations, drive innovation, and increase competitiveness. However, this proposal has faced opposition from Parliament's Portfolio Committee on Communications and Digital Technologies, which believes that privatization should not be a foregone conclusion.

Withdrawal of the SABC Bill

Malatsi withdrew the South African Broadcasting Corporation (SABC) Bill, which aimed to overhaul the Broadcasting Act and address the public broadcaster's financial woes. He cited concerns that the Bill did not adequately address the SABC's sustainability and could erode its independence by granting the Minister additional powers overboard appointments. Instead, he prioritized developing a credible funding model to ensure the SABC's long-term viability.

DA's Draft Policy Engagement

As the Democratic Alliance's national spokesperson, Malatsi has been involved in the party's initiative to open its draft policies for public scrutiny and input. This unprecedented step aims to engage South Africans in shaping the party's values, principles, and policy positions ahead of its policy conference.

These policy directions reflect Malatsi's commitment to leveraging digital technologies for economic empowerment, modernizing state-owned entities, and ensuring the sustainability of public broadcasting in South Africa
