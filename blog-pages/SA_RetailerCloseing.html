Business Top South African retailer closing stores in Gauteng

June 5, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

SPAR is continuing to shrink its presence in South Africa, closing more stores to focus on core operations. The group reported a R4 billion loss for the six months ending March 2025, mainly due to major impairments in its European businesses.

Following an expensive exit from Poland, SPAR is working to exit Switzerland and the UK, where it recorded R4.4 billion in losses, including R4.2 billion in impairments.

Its Southern Africa operations performed better, with a 1.7% revenue rise and R768 million profit after tax. Most of its business remains in South Africa, with modest wholesale and retail revenue growth of 1.1% and 1.9%, respectively. Growth was hampered by low food inflation, unrest in Mozambique, and store closures in Gauteng.

Despite acquiring four stores, SPAR closed a net of five stores between September 2024 and March 2025 and nine since January. Total stores in Southern Africa dropped to 2,021 by March.

Still, the group is investing in store upgrades, with 84 refurbishments underway, costing R211 million, and its total selling space is growing, now at 1.39 million square metres.
