The Hidden Cost of Poor Contracts in Modern Marketing

In today's fast-changing marketing landscape, contracts are no longer just legal documents filed away and forgotten. They've become critical tools for financial control and accountability. That was the key message from the latest Media Marketing Compliance (MCC) Masterclass, hosted by the Independent Agency Search & Selection Company (IAS).

The session brought marketing procurement professionals face to face with a reality that's becoming hard to ignore: contracts simply aren't keeping up with the rapid pace of industry change. Stephen Broderick, Senior Partner at MCC, and Jane Dormer, Client Services Director, led the session and highlighted just how big the challenge has become.

Broderick pointed out that contracts are often filled with grey areas, many of which aren't immediately obvious. If no one is monitoring them closely, it's all too easy for costs to quietly slip through the cracks.

Not long ago, marketing teams worked with a handful of partners — media, PR, creative, and maybe an activation agency. Contracts were often brief, and in some cases, not even signed. But the industry has since exploded in complexity.

The 2010s introduced principal-based media buying, programmatic trading, social platforms, and the unbundling of content production. Now in the 2020s, marketers are also navigating influencer deals, e-commerce platforms, affiliate networks, and retail media. The arrival of AI has added even more complications, especially around issues like intellectual property, data ownership, and compliance with third-party platforms.

As a result, the number and length of contracts have skyrocketed. What used to be simple agreements are now 100-page legal documents. And while contracts have gotten more complex, procurement teams have not grown at the same pace. Dormer noted that budgets and staffing haven't kept up, forcing teams to prioritise which contracts get the attention they deserve.

At the same time, the traditional business model that agencies relied on — generating profit through high-volume media buying — has collapsed. Tech giants like Google, Meta, and Amazon have disrupted the space, while retail media is increasingly cutting agencies out of the picture.

With pressure mounting, many agencies still need to hit historical profit margins. That's led to a troubling trend: exploiting unclear contract terms and taking advantage of client blind spots wherever possible.

One example shows just how serious the issue has become. A recent ANA study on programmatic media revealed that 70% of ad spend — including agency fees — didn't lead to ads being seen by actual people. That's not efficient advertising; that's a very expensive game of digital hide and seek.

Research from MCC shows that 62% of advertisers say the biggest barrier to managing contracts effectively is simply a lack of time. It's a vicious cycle — as contracts grow more complicated, teams have even less time to understand and manage them properly. And those blind spots are exactly where value is lost.

The way forward doesn't require reinvention, but it does call for a shift in mindset. Clients need to expect more: more clarity, more evidence, and stronger compliance. That means moving away from a mindset of chasing refunds and toward one focused on building value. It also means choosing partners who are transparent and investing in systems that can monitor contracts in real time.

Johanna McDowell, CEO of IAS, said the time for these conversations is long overdue. The masterclass was commissioned because far too many marketers are unknowingly bleeding value due to poor contract visibility. This isn't just about clawing back lost profit — it's about creating fair, modern partnerships that reflect the complex marketing ecosystem we live in today.

The message is clear: take a step back and take a serious look at your contracts. Because in a world where margins are shrinking and agency models are under pressure, the real power could be in the fine print. The era of treating contracts as a low priority is over.

The real question now isn't whether your procurement team can afford to focus on contracts — it's whether you can afford not to.

The Independent Agency Search & Selection Company (IAS) was established in South Africa in 2006 in partnership with the UK-based AAR Group, a company with more than 40 years of experience and a global presence. IAS specialises in helping clients manage agency relationships and select the right partners, following international and local best practice guidelines set by the Institute of Practitioners in Advertising (IPA) in the UK and the Association of Communications Agencies (ACA) in South Africa.
