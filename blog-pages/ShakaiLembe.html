Media Shaka iLembe: More Than a Series It’s Cultural Preservation

June 13, 2025 ● Uhone Kwinda

Shaka Zulu, born around 1787, is one of South Africa’s most iconic and complex figures. His story has been told in many forms, but often through distorted lenses. The 2023 drama series Shaka iLembe stands out for its rich storytelling and cultural authenticity and now, it’s back for a second season.

As experts in isiZulu and Zulu culture, we analysed season one to see just how accurate it was. What we found was impressive: the series uses authentic language, dress, and traditions that not only entertain but also preserve Zulu heritage.

Language and Culture Go Hand in Hand Shaka iLembe uses isiZulu in powerful ways through proverbs, idioms, and respectful (hlonipha) language. These aren’t just decorative they carry deep cultural meaning. For example, when Shaka faces rejection, his aunt comforts him with the proverb: “insimbi ibunjwa ngokugazingwa” (hardships make you stronger).

Authentic Dress and Representation Costumes also reflect real Zulu customs. Leopard skin, for instance, is worn only by royalty, correcting the stereotype that all Zulus dressed the same.

Why It Matters

TV influences culture. By bringing isiZulu and Zulu customs to mainstream media, Shaka iLembe helps keep traditions alive for younger generations in a world where many indigenous languages are fading.

Stories like this are not just entertainment they’re a vital tool for cultural preservation.
